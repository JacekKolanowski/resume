About

I am a recognised leader, educator and expert in interdisciplinary research across the fields of molecular probes, biosensing, biophysical chemistry, imaging and chemical biology.

My strong experience in managing and conducting highly innovative research has earned me the position of the youngest group leader in history at my current Institute and allowed me to attract significant national funding.

I want to further my contributions in unveiling the molecular mysteries of disease by leading my own research group and participating in scientific outreach and policy-making.

Experience

Director

Centre for High-Throughput Screening Studies (Institute of Bioorganic Chemistry PAS and Centre for Advanced Technology)

Coordinating the development and activity of the interdisciplinary research centre composed of 3 laboratories and multiple biological users, shaping research agenda of the Centre, representing the Centre in pan-European EU-OPENSCREEN ERIC consortium (over 20 institutions in 8 countries) and its national branch – POL-OPENSCREEN, co-authoring and coordinating (as work package leader) an international grant (with over 30 partners from 16 countries), managing of infrastructural grant from Polish Ministry of Science and Higher Education (almost 5,000,000 AUD equivalent per 5 years)

Principal Investigator

Department of Molecular Probes and Prodrugs (Institute of Bioorganic Chemistry Polish Academy of Sciences, Poznań, Poland)

Establishing and managing a new research group in biophysical chemistry (e.g. dual-analyte fluorescent probes, bioluminogenic substrates, probes for covalent tagging of proteins in situ and measuring of their local biochemical microenvironment, biochemical and cellular assay development, high throughput screening assays and probes), supervision of 2 postdocs, 2 PhD students, 2 MSc students and 3 undergraduates, writing and management of 3 research grants for a total of over 1.1 million AUD equivalent.
